Methodology Note

Football-specific donor vs. institutional breakdown is estimated by applying football's share of total athletic spending proportionally to department-wide donor contributions and institutional support. Actual per-sport funding allocations are not publicly reported in NCAA MFRS data.

About This Data

📊

Data Source

All financial data originates from the NCAA Membership Financial Reporting System (MFRS), compiled by the Knight-Newhouse College Athletics Database. Data is for FY 2024 (2023-24 academic year).

🏈

Football Estimates

Football spending is reported as a total by the NCAA. We estimate football's share of donor and institutional funding by proportionally allocating department-wide revenue sources based on football's percentage of total athletic spending.

⚠

Limitations

Most private schools are not legally required to disclose MFRS data. Ivy League data is compiled from EADA filings and university financial reports. Ivy schools are FCS (not FBS), don't offer athletic scholarships, and sponsor 30-40+ sports, so football's share of spending is much lower than at FBS programs.
